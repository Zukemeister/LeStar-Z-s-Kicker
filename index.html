LeStar Z's Kicker

This Kicker, like all of the LeStar Z apps, is designed to help you learn. You can't use it in games, but you can take the knowledge you gain from it to those games. To use it effectively:

  1. Put your phone with the app showing on the table.
  2. Set the cue ball on the table and on the app to about the same position.
  3. Choose a desired point on the table that the cue ball needs to go — to pocket an object ball, to kick an opponent's ball away, to play a safe.
  4. Then add as many rails as necessary for the cue ball to get to that position.
  5. Grab the endpoint of the last segment.
  6. Put it on the target location.
  7. The diagram will show you the path.
  8. The readouts will tell you the speed and spin you need to get there.
  9. Experiment and see if you can adjust yourself and the bias slider so that you can consistently calculate the shot.

AIM HANDLE (gold ●): Drag the white cue ball to your position on the table. Drag the gold handle to set your aim direction. The gold line shows the ball's path to the first rail contact.

BOUNCE CHAIN: Each rail contact continues in a new color — blue, red, green, violet. Use ＋ ADD RAIL to extend the chain up to 5 rails; － REMOVE RAIL to trim it back.

ENDPOINT HANDLE (white ●): The white dot at the end of the last segment is draggable. Pull it to any spot on the table and the app reverse-solves the aim angle to deliver the ball there. All segments update live. This answers "how do I aim to get it there?"

ROTATE ⟳: The button on the table corner cycles through 0° / 90° / 180° / 270°. Useful for matching the table orientation to your view at the table. All dragging works at every rotation.

SPEED: Controls rail compression. Faster shots produce a shallower rebound angle. Set this to match your intended shot power.

SPIN: Right english (R) opens the rebound angle after contact; left english (L) closes it. Set spin after locking in the basic aim.

BIAS: Accounts for your specific table's rail personality. Some tables play long; some play short. Adjust after observing a few real shots on your table.

POOL / SNOOKER: Snooker tables are larger and the cushions play slightly truer — less compression at the same speed. The toggle adjusts the physics and labels the readouts accordingly.

DIAMONDS: The standard diamond reference system. 0 at each corner, 4 at the side pockets on long rails, 1–3 in between. A readout of TOP ◆ 2.50 means halfway between diamonds 2 and 3 on the top rail.
